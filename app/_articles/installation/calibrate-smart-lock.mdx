## Position Encoder Drift Mechanics and Calibration Necessity

**Stepper motor open-loop control limitations**: Smart locks predominantly implement open-loop stepper motor control (no feedback sensor verifying actual shaft position, only commanded steps counted) where controller tracks position through step accumulation: "locked" position = step 0, "unlocked" = step 200, current position = cumulative steps from reference. This open-loop architecture proves cost-effective ($2-5 per lock versus $15-25 for closed-loop encoder) yet vulnerable to step loss where commanded rotation fails achieving expected movement.

**Step loss accumulation mechanism**: Momentary load exceeding motor holding torque (typical 0.3-0.8 Nm, versus required 0.2-0.5 Nm nominal yet encountering 1-2 Nm peak during strike contact) causes rotor skip where energized coils fail advancing shaft despite step counter incrementing. Example: motor commanded "advance 5 steps" (controller increments position counter +5) yet shaft actually rotates 4 steps (strike resistance causing 1-step skip), creating +1 step cumulative error. After 100 operations with 0.5% average step loss = 50 steps accumulated error (200 steps full range Ã— 25% = 50 steps = 1/4-inch position error on typical 1-inch deadbolt travel).
